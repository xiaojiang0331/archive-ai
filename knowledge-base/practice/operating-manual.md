# Operating Manual — Standard Practice Workflow

## 1. Client onboarding and authority

**Owner:** practice manager / qualified reviewer.
**Input:** signed engagement terms, entity details, authorised contacts, access authority, privacy notice acknowledgement, required identity/risk documents.
**Output:** active client record, service scope, access matrix, review cadence, and exceptions log.

Steps:

1. Confirm legal entity, service scope, reporting periods, systems, and authorised contacts.
2. Record what the practice may access and who may approve work.
3. Identify tasks that require a registered agent or professional sign-off.
4. Create a document-retention and privacy classification; do not upload unnecessary identity/bank data.
5. Send incomplete or high-risk onboarding to a reviewer.

## 2. Document intake and triage

**Owner:** bookkeeper.
**Input:** invoice, receipt, bank statement, payroll report, BAS notice, or client note.
**Output:** immutable original file, extracted fields, classification proposal, confidence score, and review queue item when needed.

Steps:

1. Validate type, size, malware/scan status, client ownership, and retention class.
2. Preserve original plus upload time; never overwrite it with OCR output.
3. Extract supplier/customer, date, amount, tax indicator, reference number, and supporting text.
4. Propose a document type and chart-of-accounts code.
5. Route low-confidence, duplicate, missing-field, or high-risk documents to review.

## 3. Daily/weekly transaction processing

**Owner:** bookkeeper; reviewer for exceptions.
**Input:** reviewed source documents and bank feed items.
**Output:** coded transactions, matched bills/invoices, exception list, and audit trail.

Steps:

1. Match bank items to source documents; do not create a new expense if a matching bill already exists.
2. Code using the approved chart of accounts and selected GST treatment.
3. Attach source evidence and retain source/document dates separately.
4. Flag personal, capital, unusual, related-party, cash, entertainment, or unclear transactions.
5. Never approve a payment or change supplier bank details from an emailed instruction alone.

## 4. Period-end reconciliation and review

**Owner:** bookkeeper prepares; accountant/reviewer signs off.
**Input:** bank/card statements, ledger, payroll registers, AR/AP reports, inventory/loan/fixed-asset schedules where applicable.
**Output:** completed reconciliations, difference explanations, review checklist, locked workpaper version.

Steps:

1. Reconcile every bank and card account to the statement closing balance.
2. Review suspense, uncoded, duplicate, unreconciled, and stale items.
3. Reconcile control accounts to subledgers where relevant.
4. Compare month-on-month movements and flag unusual variance.
5. Prepare a dated review pack; no final reporting until open exceptions are assigned or approved.

## 5. BAS / payroll / compliance workpaper preparation

**Owner:** eligible bookkeeper/BAS agent prepares; authorised reviewer approves/lodges.
**Input:** reconciled ledger, GST coding review, payroll reports, prior-period adjustments, current ATO guidance.
**Output:** BAS workpaper, exception list, approvals, and submission evidence after lodgment.

Steps:

1. Confirm the client, period, accounting basis, source completeness, and authority.
2. Produce calculation workpapers with links to supporting transactions.
3. Identify adjustments, corrections, missing information, and uncertain tax treatment.
4. Obtain required client/professional approval before lodgment.
5. Store final lodged copy, receipt/reference, payment status, and any follow-up.

## 6. Month-end reporting and year-end handover

**Owner:** bookkeeper prepares; accountant reviews.
**Input:** reconciled period, open-item list, management-reporting requirements, prior-year workpapers.
**Output:** management pack / year-end handover pack, review notes, and action list.

Steps:

1. Freeze the reporting extract and label its period and basis.
2. Produce P&L, balance sheet, cash/AR/AP reports, and variance notes as agreed.
3. Create a handover index linking ledgers, reconciliations, schedules, and unresolved issues.
4. The accountant decides adjustments, tax treatment, statutory-reporting applicability, and sign-off.

## Exception protocol

An AI suggestion must become an exception when confidence is low, evidence conflicts, the amount is unusual, a policy rule is missing, a personal-data request is involved, or an external filing/payment is implied. The exception record needs an owner, reason, source links, deadline, and reviewer decision.
